About FUTS

This project simulates a shortwave time signal station, featuring pre-tones, continuous tones, ticks, FSK data bursts, and voice announcements — all synchronized to your device's clock.

Note: For best results, use a modern browser and allow audio playback.
Inspired by
NIST WWV
US time signal station broadcasting at 2.5, 5, 10, 15 & 20 MHz from Fort Collins, Colorado, operated by NIST since 1923. Learn more →
Inspired by
CHU Canada
Canada's official shortwave time signal station broadcast at 3.33, 7.85 & 14.67 MHz by the National Research Council, known for its bilingual voice announcements and FSK data encoding.

FSK Encoding — DUT1 Split-Second

Starting at hh:mm:30, a 2500 Hz FSK burst transmits:
"FUTS TIME SIGNAL {hour} Hour {minutes} Minutes UTC"

In addition, DUT1 (UT1 − UTC) is encoded via split seconds, mirroring the CHU Canada / NIST WWV convention:

• Positive DUT1 (+0.1 s to +0.8 s) → seconds 1 through 8 are split (doubled tick).
• Negative DUT1 (−0.1 s to −0.8 s) → seconds 9 through 16 are split (doubled tick).

During the FSK burst and all voice announcements, the once-per-second tone is reduced to a 10-millisecond tick to avoid masking the audio content.
